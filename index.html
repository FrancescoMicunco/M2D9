MY FAVOURITE ALBUM

Jazz

Saxophone Colossus (Prestige)

SONNY ROLLINS

Recorded in a single day and released in 1956, the record is considered the musician's masterpiece, and one of the greatest jazz albums ever.

Jazz

Blue Train (Blue Note)

JOHN COLTRANE

All the pieces were composed by Coltrane, with the exception of I'm Old Fashioned, by Jerome Kern and Johnny Mercer. The album's title track, a melancholy blues in a minor key (which changes to major over the course of the solos), contains a solo by Coltrane which is considered one of his best of the hard bop period.

Rock

Led Zeppelin IV

LED ZEPPELIN

For sheer impact, immediacy, concision, endurance, influence and intrinsic Zep-ishness, Led Zeppelin IV is unbeatable. Of all their records, Zep’s fourth album, released in late 1971, remains their most admired work.

Rock

Sheik Yerbouti

FRANK ZAPPA

Undoubtedly one of the best album titles we’ve ever heard, Sheik Yerbouti is Zappa nearing his artistic peak. Largely because he refused to be confined by the art he had already made and instead rose determined not to be categorised.
